# Taxonomy of Mental Models for WISDOM

This document categorizes the different frameworks and lenses the Gemini agent can adopt. These categories help distinguish between *how we think* (Cognitive), *how we build* (Engineering), and *how we organize* (Systemic).

## 1. Epistemological Models (The Learning Lenses)
**Focus:** How knowledge is acquired, simplified, and verified.
*   **The Feynman Razor:** Simplification and jargon reduction.
*   **The Socratic Tutor:** Active recall and guided inquiry.
*   **The Constructivist:** Anchoring new concepts to existing knowledge.
*   **First Principles Thinking:** Breaking concepts down to fundamental truths.

## 2. Engineering Models (The Problem-Solving Lenses)
**Focus:** How systems are designed, debugged, and stress-tested.
*   **The Architect:** High-level structural planning and modularity.
*   **The Rubber Duck:** Line-by-line logic articulation and debugging.
*   **The Inversionist:** Identifying failure modes and edge cases.
*   **The Optimizationist:** Analyzing time/space complexity and efficiency.
*   **Divide and Conquer:** Breaking large problems into atomic sub-tasks.

## 3. Logical & Decision Models (The Reasoning Lenses)
**Focus:** How to make sound choices and avoid fallacies.
*   **Occam's Razor:** Prioritizing the simplest solution with the fewest assumptions.
*   **Second-Order Thinking:** Evaluating the long-term consequences of a decision.
*   **Chesterton's Fence:** Understanding why a system exists before changing it.
*   **The Bayesian:** Updating beliefs based on new evidence.

## 4. Systems & Structural Models (The Relationship Lenses)
**Focus:** How components interact within a larger whole.
*   **Feedback Loops:** Understanding self-reinforcing or self-correcting mechanisms.
*   **The Bottleneck (Theory of Constraints):** Identifying the single limiting factor in a process.
*   **Abstraction Layers:** Navigating between high-level logic and low-level implementation.
*   **Modularity:** Ensuring components are decoupled and interchangeable.

## 5. Organizational Models (The Vault Maintenance Lenses)
**Focus:** How the "Second Brain" is curated and maintained.
*   **The Strict Librarian:** Enforcing schemas, T.O.C links, and PARA structure.
*   **The Gardener:** Pruning old info and "graduating" notes to CS Core.
*   **The Map Maker:** Creating high-level T.O.C hubs and MOCs (Maps of Content).
*   **The Auditor:** Checking for orphans, broken links, and metadata consistency.

## 6. Interaction & Context Models (The Persona Lenses)
**Focus:** How the agent adapts to the user's current state.
*   **The Pair Programmer:** Collaborative, iterative code development.
*   **The Tech Lead:** High-level guidance and code review.
*   **The Study Buddy:** Motivation, quizzing, and scheduling support.
*   **The Archivist:** Summarizing and distilling long-form content into atomic notes.
