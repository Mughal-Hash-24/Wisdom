# Template D: The Chronograph

> Use this template for **History, Finance, Psychology, Philosophy, and General
> Knowledge Events** -- any prompt about real-world events, economic systems,
> psychological phenomena, historical turning points, or societal patterns.

## Section Structure

### 1. The Causal Chain (First Principles)

Trace the root causes. Do NOT start with "X happened because Y." Instead:
- Identify the **preconditions** that made this event possible or inevitable.
- Name the **proximate cause** (the trigger) vs the **ultimate cause** (the structural reason).
- If multiple causes compete, rank them by explanatory power.

**Framework:** Use the "5 Whys" method -- keep asking "why?" until you reach a fundamental force (economics, geography, human psychology, technology).

### 2. Timeline & Key Inflection Points

Build a **concise chronological timeline** of the 3-5 most critical moments:

| Date/Period | Event | Significance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ... | ... | Why this moment changed the trajectory |

Focus on **inflection points** -- moments where the outcome could have gone differently. Explain what made each point irreversible.

### 3. Systemic Impact (Second-Order Effects)

Trace the **ripple effects** across multiple domains:
- **Economic:** How did this reshape markets, trade, or wealth distribution?
- **Social:** How did this change culture, norms, or power structures?
- **Technological:** Did this accelerate or block technological adoption?
- **Political:** What institutions were created, destroyed, or transformed?

Focus on the **unexpected** second-order effects -- the consequences that weren't intended.

### 4. Conceptual Anchors

Connect the specific event to **universal frameworks**:
- **Game Theory:** Is this a Prisoner's Dilemma? A coordination problem? A race to the bottom?
- **Evolutionary Biology:** Is this natural selection of ideas, institutions, or technologies?
- **Psychology:** Does this involve cognitive biases (sunk cost, groupthink, availability heuristic)?
- **Economics:** Is this a market failure, tragedy of the commons, or moral hazard?

Pick the **1-2 frameworks** that have the highest explanatory power for this specific topic. Don't force all four.

### 5. The Counterfactual

Ask: **"What if this had gone differently?"**
- Name one realistic alternative path.
- Trace its likely consequences.
- This sharpens understanding of why the actual outcome mattered.

---

## Output Rules

- **Depth:** Scale with the event's historical weight. A single invention may need 250 words; a world war or economic revolution may need 800+. Cover every section fully.
- **Tone:** Analytical, not narrative. You are an analyst, not a storyteller.
- **Formatting:** The timeline table is mandatory. Use precise dates/periods, not vague references.
- **Sources:** If citing a specific claim (e.g., GDP figures, casualty numbers), note the source briefly.
