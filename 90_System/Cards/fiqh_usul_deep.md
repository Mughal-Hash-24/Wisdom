# Fiqh Usul Deep Card

## Goal
Explain how this school's legal methodology (*usul al-fiqh*) applies to this category of problem — not just what the ruling is. The methodological framework is the primary deliverable. The ruling is secondary.

## Output Requirements

1. **Ruling** — state it briefly. This is not the focus.
2. **Usul al-Fiqh** — this section must be expanded significantly beyond the standard ruling card. Cover:
   - How this school's foundational usul commitments shape its approach to this *type* of question (not just this specific question)
   - Where the four recognized sources (Quran, Sunnah, Ijma, Qiyas) sit in the school's hierarchy for this category
   - The specific usul tools this school uses here (istihsan, maslaha, amal ahl al-Madina, etc.) and why
   - The epistemological assumptions beneath the methodology — why does this school consider this derivation valid?
3. **Usul-Level Disputes** — this field must be long and substantive. Engage directly with how the other three schools' usul frameworks would handle this category differently, and why.
4. **Internal Dissent** — focus on dissent driven by usul disagreement, not just outcome disagreement.

## Quality Signals
- A reader who finishes this could explain not just what the school rules, but *why this school reasons this way*
- The Usul-Level Disputes section reads like a methodological debate between traditions, not a list of outcomes
- The school's specific usul tools are named and explained (e.g., "The Maliki school applies maslaha mursala here because...")
- The epistemological stake is clear: what does each school believe about the relationship between reason and revelation?

## Anti-Patterns
- DO NOT write a standard ruling card with a slightly longer Usul section — this card requires a fundamentally different emphasis
- DO NOT reduce usul to "the school uses analogy" — name the specific tool and justify its use
- DO NOT skip the epistemological layer — why the school considers this methodology valid is as important as what the methodology produces
