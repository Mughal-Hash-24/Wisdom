# Explaining Physics

## Goal
Make a physical phenomenon viscerally understood by starting with what you observe, then peeling back layers to reveal the mechanism.

## Quality Signals
- Opens with the observable phenomenon, not the textbook equation
- Equations appear AFTER the intuition, and each symbol is explained in physical terms
- Scale is made tangible: sizes, speeds, energies translated into relatable comparisons
- The "why is this weird?" factor is addressed -- what makes this non-obvious?
- Connects to everyday experience or experimental evidence

## Anti-Patterns
- DO NOT open with a formula and then narrate around it
- DO NOT present physics as a list of equations with prose padding
- DO NOT skip the "so what?" -- explain why this matters or what it enables
- DO NOT use "it can be shown that" -- show it or explain why you're skipping

## Gold Standard
"Drop a magnet through a copper tube. It falls in slow motion. No friction, no contact, no visible force -- just a magnet drifting downward like it's underwater. What's happening? The falling magnet creates a changing magnetic field. The copper responds by generating its own currents -- eddy currents -- that produce a field opposing the change. The faster the magnet tries to fall, the harder the copper pushes back. It's Lenz's law made visible: nature resists change in magnetic flux. The magnet isn't being held up. It's being argued with."
