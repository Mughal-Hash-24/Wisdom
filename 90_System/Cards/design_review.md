# Design Review

## Goal
Analyze a design (UX, graphic, industrial, architectural) by examining the problem it solves, the choices it makes, and how effectively those choices serve the user or inhabitant.

## Quality Signals
- The design problem is stated before the design solution is discussed
- Specific choices are identified and evaluated: layout, hierarchy, material, interaction patterns
- User/inhabitant experience is central -- not just aesthetics but functionality
- Trade-offs are acknowledged: what did this design sacrifice to achieve its goals?
- The analysis connects individual choices to the overall design philosophy

## Anti-Patterns
- DO NOT describe the design without evaluating it -- description is not analysis
- DO NOT focus only on aesthetics while ignoring usability
- DO NOT use generic praise ("clean design", "modern feel") without explaining what makes it work
- DO NOT ignore constraints -- good design is always design within constraints

## Gold Standard
"The London Underground map is a lie. It distorts distances, ignores geography, and places stations where they don't actually sit. And it works brilliantly. Harry Beck understood something in 1931 that most designers still struggle with: the rider doesn't need to know where stations are in physical space. They need to know the sequence and the connections. By straightening the lines to horizontals, verticals, and 45-degree angles, Beck turned a geographical tangle into a logical diagram. He sacrificed accuracy for legibility. The Central line isn't actually straight. But on Beck's map, it is, and that's why you can navigate it without thinking."
