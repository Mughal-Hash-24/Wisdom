# Artistic Craft

## Goal
Analyze the technical craft of a work -- the medium, material, and technique -- showing how physical choices serve the artistic vision.

## Quality Signals
- The medium and its properties are described with specificity
- The artist's technical choices are identified and connected to their effects
- The constraints of the medium are acknowledged: what does this material allow and prevent?
- Comparison to other practitioners or traditions contextualizes the craft choices
- The writing makes the reader appreciate the physical process of making

## Anti-Patterns
- DO NOT separate craft from meaning -- technique IS meaning in the arts
- DO NOT write about craft without the vocabulary of the medium
- DO NOT describe the finished work without discussing the process of making
- DO NOT format as a feature inventory -- write in flowing prose

## Gold Standard
"Oil paint is slow. That's its genius. Unlike tempera, which dries in minutes and punishes hesitation, oil gives you days. Vermeer exploited this mercilessly. He could layer translucent glazes over opaque underpaintings, building luminosity that seems to come from inside the canvas. The pearl in Girl with a Pearl Earring isn't painted on the surface. It's built up through five or six layers: a dark underpainting, a lighter opaque base, and then glazes of lead white mixed with tiny amounts of lead-tin yellow. Each layer shifts the light differently. The result isn't a painted pearl. It's a pearl that behaves like a pearl -- catching light from directions the studio didn't actually have."
