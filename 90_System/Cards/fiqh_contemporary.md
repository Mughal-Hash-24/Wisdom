# Fiqh Contemporary Card

## Goal
Apply this school's classical methodology to a modern or novel situation that has no direct classical precedent. Every output under this card is a derived position — the school's jurists did not rule on this specific case. The qiyas chain from classical asl to modern far' is the core deliverable.

## Output Requirements

1. **Query Type** — MUST be marked `[DERIVED POSITION]`. No exceptions.
2. **Ruling** — this is the school's *derived* position, constructed by extending classical principles. State it clearly, but acknowledge it as derived: "Based on the principles of X, the Hanafi school would likely hold that..."
3. **Usul al-Fiqh** — MUST use the Qiyas template. All four components are mandatory:
   - **Asl** (Original Case): the closest classical case with a known ruling
   - **Far'** (New Case): the modern situation being ruled on
   - **Illah** (Effective Cause): the shared property that connects them — this must be argued, not assumed
   - **Hukm** (Extended Ruling): the ruling carried from Asl to Far'
   - **Why This Illah**: why the school's methodology validates this particular illah — this is where schools diverge most
4. **Contemporary Disputes** (replaces Usul-Level Disputes): How do contemporary jurists within this school disagree on whether and how to apply classical principles to this case?
5. **Internal Dissent** — are there contemporary scholars in this school who reject the qiyas entirely, or propose a different Asl?

## Quality Signals
- The Asl is a real classical case with a clear ruling — not invented
- The Illah is argued, not stated as obvious
- The derived ruling is presented with appropriate epistemic humility ("would likely", "the strongest argument within this school is...")
- No classical citation is fabricated for the modern case itself — the contemporary case has no classical hadith or Quran verse directly addressing it

## Anti-Patterns
- DO NOT mark the output as `[CLASSICAL POSITION]` — this is always a derived position
- DO NOT cite a classical hadith or Quran verse as directly addressing the modern case (e.g., there is no hadith about cryptocurrency)
- DO NOT skip the Illah justification — "X and Y are similar" is not an argument
- DO NOT fabricate a named contemporary scholar's position — attribute only to "contemporary jurists within the school" if uncertain
- DO NOT present the derived ruling with the same confidence as a classical position
