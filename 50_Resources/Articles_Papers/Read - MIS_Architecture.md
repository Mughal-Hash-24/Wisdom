## Definition
**Management Information System (MIS)** is a Computer Based Information System (CBIS) designed to meet the general information needs of managers in an organization. It makes information available to users with similar needs.

## The Three Pillars
MIS consists of three core components:

1.  **Management**: The art of getting things done through people in formally organized groups.
    *   *Functions*: Planning, Organizing, Staffing, Directing, Controlling.
2.  **Information**: Data that has meaning within a context (where data is raw facts about an entity).
3.  **System**: A set of inter-related components with a clearly defined boundary working together to achieve a common goal.

## Strategic Importance
Information systems are as vital as Marketing, Finance, or HR. They play three key roles:
1.  Support business processes and operations.
2.  Support decision-making by employees and managers.
3.  Support strategies for competitive advantage.

## Advantages & Disadvantages

| Advantages | Disadvantages |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Decision Quality**: Improves organization by providing relevant info. | **Rigidity**: Can be too rigid and difficult to adapt. |
| **Summarization**: Condenses large data sets to avoid confusion. | **Resistance**: Internal friction in sharing info reduces effectiveness. |
| **Integration**: Keeps departments aware of each other's requirements. | **Quantification**: Hard to quantify benefits to justify implementation. |
| **Linkage**: Connects planning and control, improving evaluation. | **GIGO**: Quality of output depends on quality of input/process. |

## Organizational Systems (Management Levels)
Organizational information systems are logical ways of thinking about MIS, divided into levels:

1.  **Strategic Planning Level**: Plan
2.  **Management Control Level**: Organize
3.  **Operational Control Level**: Direct

### Operational Control Domains
*   **Marketing**: Applying computers to the entire range of marketing operations.
*   **Finance**: Computer-based systems for payroll, taxation, etc.
*   **Human Resource**: HRIS and HRMS for managing human capital.
*   **Manufacturing**: Information collection, quality control, and cost control.
*   **Information Resource**: Managing the technology (conceptual and physical) itself.
