# Hanbali Position: What is the ruling on the five daily prayers?
[[T.O.C (Fiqh)|Up to Fiqh]] | [[Synthesis - five-daily-prayers-ruling|Back to Synthesis]]

**School:** Hanbali
**Methodological Disposition:** Textualist. Strongest reliance on hadith corpus, including weak narrations; most resistant to independent qiyas and maslaha.
**Query Type:** CLASSICAL POSITION

---

## Ruling

The five daily prayers are **Fard 'Ayn** (an individual obligation) upon every sane, pubescent Muslim. Neglecting them is considered one of the greatest sins, and the Hanbali school holds the strictest position regarding the legal status of one who abandons them.

**Classical Reference:** *Al-Mughni* by Ibn Qudama (VERIFIED); *Sharh al-Muntaha* by Al-Buhuti (VERIFIED).

---

## Usul al-Fiqh — How This Ruling Was Derived

**Primary Legal Source:** Quran, Sunnah, and Ijma.

**Derivation Chain:**

*If Quran:*
- **Verse:** Surah Al-Baqarah (Q 2:43) — "And establish prayer and give zakah and bow with those who bow [in worship and obedience]." Also Al-Nisa (Q 4:103) — "Indeed, prayer has been decreed upon the believers a decree of specified times."
- **Asbab al-Nuzul:** While specific verses regarding prayer times had various contexts, the command to establish prayer is a foundational directive repeated throughout the Meccan and Medinan periods.
- **Textual Analysis:**
  - *Muhkam or Mutashabih:* **Muhkam**. The command "Aqimu" (establish) is a definitive imperative (Amr).
  - *Amm or Khass:* **Amm**. It applies to all believers who meet the conditions of Taklif (legal responsibility).
  - *Nasikh/Mansukh:* No abrogation applies here. The obligation remained fixed from the night of Isra and Mi'raj until the end of revelation.
- **Juristic Reading:** The Hanbali school applies the *Zahir* (literal) meaning of the imperative "Aqimu." In Hanbali Usul, an absolute command (Amr Mutlaq) necessitates immediate and recurring obligation (Wujub) unless a secondary evidence (Sarin) diverts it, which does not exist here.

*If Sunnah:*
- **Hadith:** "Islam is built upon five: testifying that there is no god but Allah and that Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah, establishing prayer..." — narrated by Ibn Umar, reported in Bukhari and Muslim (VERIFIED).
- **Authentication Status:** **Sahih**. This is a Mutawatir (mass-narrated) concept in the Sunnah.
- **Asbab al-Wurud:** The Prophet (peace be upon him) frequently instructed delegations (such as the delegation of 'Abd al-Qays) that the first duty after Tawhid is the five prayers.
- **Scope:** **Aam**. The Prophet defined these five prayers as the boundary between belief and disbelief.
- **Application:** The Hanbali school utilizes the Hadith of the Night Journey (Isra) to fix the number at five and the Hadith of Gabriel to fix the timings.

*If Ijma:*
- **Consensus Body:** Companions and all subsequent generations.
- **Period:** Since the lifetime of the Prophet (pbuh).
- **Scope:** Universal across all schools.
- **Note:** There is no disagreement among Muslims that the five prayers are obligatory. The only Hanbali-specific distinction lies in the consequence of abandonment.

**Usul-Level Disputes:**
The Hanbali school diverges from the Hanafi school in the classification of "Fard" vs "Wajib." While Hanafis distinguish between what is proven by definitive evidence (Fard) and speculative evidence (Wajib), Hanbalis (like Shafi'is) treat Fard and Wajib as synonymous in the context of ritual worship. Furthermore, the Hanbali school is unique in its literal application of hadiths that label the abandonment of prayer as *Kufr* (disbelief), whereas other schools typically interpret such hadiths metaphorically as "ingratitude" or "minor kufr" unless the person denies the obligation itself.

---

## Internal Dissent

The primary point of internal discussion in the Hanbali school is not the obligation itself, but the ruling on the **Tarik al-Salat** (one who leaves prayer). 
- **Dominant Position (Mu'tamad):** One who leaves even a single prayer intentionally until its time has completely passed (including the time of its joined prayer, if applicable) is considered a *Kafir* (disbeliever) who must be asked to repent. This is the view of Imam Ahmad in many reports and is supported by Ibn Taymiyya.
- **Minority/Alternative Report:** Some Hanbali scholars (and one report from Ahmad) suggest that the person is a *Fasiq* (grave sinner) but not a disbeliever, provided they acknowledge the obligation but omit it due to laziness. This aligns more closely with the Shafi'i and Maliki positions.

---

## Notes

In modern Saudi Arabia and Qatar, the Hanbali-based legal frameworks emphasize the communal importance of the five prayers. The Hanbali school's insistence on the five prayers as the defining pillar of a Muslim's identity is a hallmark of its "Athari" (textualist) orientation, preferring the literal weight of the warning hadiths over the rationalist interpretations (Ta'wil) favored by other schools.
