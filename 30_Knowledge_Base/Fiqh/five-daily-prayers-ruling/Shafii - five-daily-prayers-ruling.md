# Shafi'i Position: What is the ruling on the five daily prayers?
[[T.O.C (Fiqh)|Up to Fiqh]] | [[Synthesis - five-daily-prayers-ruling|Back to Synthesis]]

**School:** Shafi'i
**Methodological Disposition:** Systematic textualist. Al-Shafi'i formalized usul al-fiqh itself; strict qiyas within bounded methodology; skeptical of maslaha mursala and istihsan.
**Query Type:** [CLASSICAL POSITION]

---

## Ruling

The performance of the five daily prayers (Fajr, Zuhr, 'Asr, Maghrib, and 'Isha) is **Fard 'Ayn** (an individual, personal obligation) upon every "Mukallaf" — a Muslim who is sane and has reached the age of puberty (bulugh). Neglecting them out of denial of their obligation constitutes apostasy (*kufr*), while neglecting them out of laziness while acknowledging their obligation is a major sin (*kabira*) which, in the classical Shafi'i view, warrants legal intervention.

**Classical Reference:** Al-Nawawi, *Minhaj al-Talibin*; Al-Shafi'i, *Al-Umm* (VERIFIED).

---

## Usul al-Fiqh — How This Ruling Was Derived

**Primary Legal Source:** Quran, Sunnah, and Ijma (Consensus).

**Derivation Chain:**

*If Quran:*
- **Verse:** Surah al-Nisa (Q 4:103) — "...Indeed, prayer has been decreed upon the believers a decree of specified times."
- **Asbab al-Nuzul:** This verse was revealed in the context of the "Prayer of Fear" (*Salat al-Khawf*) during military expeditions, emphasizing that even in states of danger, the timing of prayer is fixed and cannot be ignored.
- **Textual Analysis:**
  - *Muhkam or Mutashabih:* This is **Muhkam** (clear and unequivocal) in establishing the obligation and the necessity of specific timings.
  - *Amm or Khass:* It is **Amm** (general) for all believers across all times and places.
  - *Nasikh/Mansukh:* No abrogation applies here; the obligation remained fixed from the Night Journey (Isra' wal-Mi'raj) until the end of the Prophetic mission.
- **Juristic Reading:** Al-Shafi'i utilizes this verse to establish that prayer is not merely a spiritual suggestion but a legal "Kitab" (prescribed law) with rigid temporal boundaries.

*If Sunnah:*
- **Hadith:** "Five prayers which Allah has prescribed upon His servants..." — reported in the context of the Bedouin (Talha bin Ubaydullah) questioning the Prophet (s) (VERIFIED).
- **Authentication Status:** **Sahih**. Reported in Sahih al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim.
- **Asbab al-Wurud:** A man from Najd asked about the requirements of Islam; the Prophet (s) listed the five prayers. When asked "Is there anything else?" the Prophet (s) replied "No, unless you volunteer." 
- **Scope:** The Shafi'i school treats this as **Amm** (general) and definitive. It establishes both the count (five) and the fact that anything beyond them is *Nafila* (voluntary).
- **Application:** This hadith serves as the primary evidence for the Shafi'i definition of "Fard," distinguishing it from the "Witr" prayer (which the Hanafis consider Wajib but Shafi'is consider Sunnah Mu'akkadah).

*If Ijma:*
- **Consensus Body:** The Companions, the Tabi'un, and all generations of jurists.
- **Period:** Established since the first year of the Hijra.
- **Scope:** Universal across all Muslim schools of thought.
- **Note:** This is an example of "Ma'lum min al-din bi al-darurah" (that which is known of the religion by necessity); denying it is considered outside the fold of Islam.

**Usul-Level Disputes:**
A key methodological distinction in the Shafi'i school is the rejection of the Hanafi distinction between *Fard* (proven by definitive/qat'i evidence) and *Wajib* (proven by speculative/zanni evidence). For Al-Shafi'i, if a command is established via a Sahih Hadith (even an Ahad/singular report), it constitutes a *Fard* obligation just as if it were in the Quran. Thus, the five prayers are Fard in the highest sense.

---

## Internal Dissent

There is no dissent regarding the **obligation** of the five prayers. However, there is a famous distinction between Al-Shafi'i's **Qawl Qadim** (Old Position) and **Qawl Jadid** (New Position) regarding the *end* of the time for Maghrib prayer:
- **Qawl Jadid (New Position):** The time for Maghrib is extremely short—only long enough to perform wudu, cover the aurah, and pray five rak'ahs. 
- **Qawl Qadim (Old Position):** The time extends until the disappearance of the red twilight (*al-shafaq al-ahmar*). 
- **Note:** Most later Shafi'i scholars (including Al-Nawawi) actually gave fatwa on the **Old Position** in this specific instance because the evidence from the Sunnah for the extended time was deemed stronger.

---

## Notes
In Shafi'i jurisprudence, the intention (*niyyah*) is a pillar (*rukn*) of the prayer itself, not just a condition. Therefore, for the prayer to be validly "performed" in fulfillment of the obligation, the specific prayer (e.g., Zuhr) must be present in the mind at the moment of the *Takbirat al-Ihram*.
