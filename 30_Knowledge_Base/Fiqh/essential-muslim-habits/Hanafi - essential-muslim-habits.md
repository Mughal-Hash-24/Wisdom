# Hanafi Position: What are the most important habits a Muslim should build?
[[T.O.C (Fiqh)|Up to Fiqh]] | [[Synthesis - essential-muslim-habits|View Synthesis]]

**School:** Hanafi
**Methodological Disposition:** Rationalist. Heaviest use of qiyas and istihsan of all four schools.
**Query Type:** CLASSICAL POSITION

---

## Ruling

The most important habits for a Muslim are structured according to a strict legal hierarchy unique to the Hanafi school. One must prioritize habits in the following order:
1. **Fard (Absolute Obligation):** Habits whose denial constitutes unbelief, established by definitive evidence (*Qat'i*). This includes the five daily prayers, fasting Ramadan, and Zakat.
2. **Wajib (Necessity):** Habits established by speculative evidence (*Zanni*). In practice, they are treated as obligatory, but their denial does not constitute unbelief. This includes the Witr prayer and the Eid prayers.
3. **Sunnah Mu'akkadah (Emphasized Sunnah):** Habits the Prophet ﷺ performed consistently. Persistence in omitting these is considered sinful or blameworthy (*is-ah*).

**Classical Reference:** *Al-Hidaya* by Al-Marghinani (VERIFIED); *Nur al-Idah* by Al-Shurunbulali (VERIFIED).

## Usul al-Fiqh — How This Ruling Was Derived

**Primary Legal Source:** Sunnah and Quranic Textual Classification.

**Derivation Chain:**

*If Quran:*
- **Verse:** Al-Baqarah (Q 2:43) — "And establish prayer and give zakat..."
- **Asbab al-Nuzul:** Revealed as general commands to the nascent Muslim community in Medina to establish the foundational structures of faith.
- **Textual Analysis:**
  - *Muhkam or Mutashabih:* Muhkam (Clear). The command "Aqimu" (Establish) is an imperative (*Amr*).
  - *Amm or Khass:* Amm (General) to all believers.
  - *Nasikh/Mansukh:* No abrogation applies to the foundational obligations of Salat and Zakat.
- **Juristic Reading:** The Hanafi school views the imperative form (*Amr*) in a definitive text (*Qat'i al-Thubut*) as establishing a *Fard* (Absolute Obligation).

*If Sunnah:*
- **Hadith:** "The Witr is a duty (haqq), so whoever does not pray Witr is not of us." — narrated by Abu Hurayrah, reported in Abu Dawud (VERIFIED)
- **Authentication Status:** Sahih/Hasan, but classified as *Ahad* (individual chain) rather than *Mutawatir* (mass-transmitted).
- **Asbab al-Wurud:** The Prophet ﷺ encouraged the Sahaba to seal their night prayers with Witr.
- **Scope:** General command, but the quality of the evidence is *Zanni* (speculative) compared to the Quran.
- **Application:** Because the evidence is *Ahad* and not *Mutawatir*, the Hanafi school classifies Witr as *Wajib* rather than *Fard*. This distinction is a hallmark of Hanafi Usul, separating them from the Shafi'i school which collapses Wajib into Fard.

*If Qiyas:*
- **Original Case (Asl):** The Pillars of a Structure.
- **New Case (Far'):** The categorization of daily habits.
- **Effective Cause (Illah):** The necessity of structural integrity vs. functional completion.
- **Extended Ruling (Hukm):** Just as a house cannot stand without pillars (Fard), it is not habitable or secure without walls (Wajib) and is not beautiful or complete without finishing touches (Sunnah).
- **Why This Illah:** The Hanafi school accepts this rational stratification because it allows for a nuanced legal response to omissions. It protects the core (*Asl*) of the faith from being equated with secondary (though important) practices.

**Usul-Level Disputes:**
The Jumhur (majority/other schools) disagree with the Hanafi distinction between *Fard* and *Wajib*. They argue that any command from Allah or the Messenger constitutes an obligation. The Hanafi school diverges here on the grounds of *Epistemological Certainty*: they refuse to equate a ruling derived from an individual report (*Ahad*) with one derived from a definitive Quranic verse, as the legal consequences (takfir for denial) must only rest on absolute certainty.

---

## Internal Dissent

A significant point of internal discussion exists regarding the status of the Sunnah of Fajr. While most *Sunan Mu'akkadah* are highly recommended, the two rak'ahs before Fajr are held in such high regard by Imam Abu Hanifa and his students that some later Hanafis argued they approach the level of *Wajib* in terms of the necessity of performance, though they remain technically Sunnah.

---

## Notes

In South Asian and Turkish Hanafi practice, the distinction of *Wajib* is most visible in the *Witr* prayer, which is prayed with three rak'ahs and a closing Takbir, a specific form derived from the school's synthesis of various reports. Habitual consistency in *Sunnah Mu'akkadah* is seen as the "protective fence" around the *Fara'id*.
