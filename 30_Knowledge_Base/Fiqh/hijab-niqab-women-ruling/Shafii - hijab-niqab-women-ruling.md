# Shafi'i Position: What are the rulings on hijab and nikab for women with exact rulings on the fard, the sunnah etc?
[[T.O.C (Fiqh)|Up to Fiqh]] | [[Synthesis - hijab-niqab-women-ruling|Back to Synthesis]]

**School:** Shafi'i
**Methodological Disposition:** Systematic textualist. Al-Shafi'i formalized usul al-fiqh itself; strict qiyas within bounded methodology; skeptical of maslaha mursala and istihsan.
**Query Type:** CLASSICAL POSITION

---

## Ruling

In the Shafi'i school, the ruling is divided between the *awrah* (nakedness) in prayer and the *awrah* in the presence of non-mahram (marriageable) men:

1.  **General Hijab (Body):** It is **Obligatory (Fard)** to cover the entire body except the face and hands in all circumstances. 
2.  **Niqab (Face and Hands):** The **dominant (mu'tamad)** position of the later Shafi'i school (as codified by Al-Nawawi and Ibn Hajar al-Haytami) is that it is **Obligatory (Wajib/Fard)** for a woman to cover her face and hands in front of non-mahram men. 
3.  **In Prayer (Salat):** The face and hands are **not** awrah; they must remain uncovered (unless a non-mahram man is present, in which case the face should be screened).

**Classical Reference:** *Minhaj al-Talibin*, Al-Nawawi; *Tuhfat al-Muhtaj*, Ibn Hajar al-Haytami (VERIFIED).

---

## Usul al-Fiqh — How This Ruling Was Derived

**Primary Legal Source:** Quran and Sunnah.

**Derivation Chain:**

*If Quran:*
- **Verse:** Surah Al-Ahzab (Q 33:59) — "O Prophet, tell your wives and your daughters and the women of the believers to draw their cloaks (jalabib) close round them..."
- **Asbab al-Nuzul:** Revealed to distinguish free believing women from others and to protect them from harassment in the streets of Madinah.
- **Textual Analysis:**
  - *Muhkam or Mutashabih:* Muhkam regarding the obligation of the *jilbab*.
  - *Amm or Khass:* Amm (General) for all believing women.
  - *Nasikh/Mansukh:* No abrogation applies; this verse is considered the final legislation on the outward appearance.
- **Juristic Reading:** Shafi'i jurists interpret "draw their cloaks close" (idna') as lowering the garment from the head over the face. While Surah An-Nur (24:31) mentions "except what is apparent thereof," the Shafi'i school (in its mu'tamad position) limits this "apparent" exception to the state of prayer or necessity (like medical treatment), while Al-Ahzab sets the standard for public life.

*If Sunnah:*
- **Hadith:** "When a woman reaches the age of menstruation, it is not correct that anything should be seen of her except this and this — and he pointed to his face and hands." — narrated by Aisha, reported in Abu Dawud (VERIFIED).
- **Authentication Status:** Mursal (disconnected chain) according to Abu Dawud, but the Shafi'i school uses it to define the *awrah* for **prayer**. 
- **Asbab al-Wurud:** Asma bint Abi Bakr entered upon the Prophet wearing thin clothes.
- **Scope:** The school distinguishes between the *awrah* of prayer and the *awrah* of "looking" (nazar). The hadith defines what is inherently not part of the body's nakedness, but other evidence (the command of jilbab and the prohibition of looking) establishes the obligation to cover these parts in public to prevent *fitnah*.
- **Application:** The hadith establishes that the face and hands are not *awrah* in essence (allowing them to be uncovered in salat), but the requirement to cover them in public is a secondary obligation based on the closing of means to temptation (*sadd al-dhara'i*).

**Usul-Level Disputes:**
The Shafi'i school is more restrictive than the Hanafi and Maliki schools on this point. Hanafis and Malikis generally view the "apparent" exception in 24:31 as a permanent license to show the face and hands in public unless *fitnah* is specifically feared. The Shafi'i school, following Al-Shafi'i’s emphasis on the Sunnah's role in specifying the Quran, argues that the prophetic practice and the specific command in Al-Ahzab necessitate covering the face as a default rule of modesty.

---

## Internal Dissent

**Minority Position:** There is a significant secondary opinion within the school (the *wajh* of some earlier Shafi'i jurists) stating that covering the face and hands is **Sunnah (Recommended)** rather than Fard, provided there is no fear of *fitnah*. This view aligns more closely with the "Old Position" (*Qawl Qadim*) and is often cited by modern Shafi'i scholars in the West or Southeast Asia to allow for flexibility. However, the *Mu'tamad* (relied-upon) position in the major commentaries remains that it is obligatory in the presence of non-mahram men.

---

## Notes

The distinction between *awrah* in prayer and *awrah* in public is a hallmark of Shafi'i precision. While a woman's prayer is valid with her face uncovered, her public presence requires the *niqab* according to the standard school texts like *Fath al-Wahhab*. In modern Southeast Asian Shafi'i practice (Indonesia/Malaysia), the secondary opinion (recommended) is widely followed, whereas in traditional Hadrami (Yemeni) Shafi'i circles, the obligatory view is strictly maintained.
