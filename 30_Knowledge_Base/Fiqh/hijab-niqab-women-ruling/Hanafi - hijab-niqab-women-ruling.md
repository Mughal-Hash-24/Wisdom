# Hanafi Position: What are the rulings on hijab and nikab for women with exact rulings on the fard, the sunnah etc?
[[T.O.C (Fiqh)|Up to Fiqh]] | [[Synthesis - hijab-niqab-women-ruling|Back to Synthesis]]

**School:** Hanafi
**Methodological Disposition:** Rationalist. Heaviest use of qiyas and istihsan of all four schools.
**Query Type:** CLASSICAL POSITION

---

## Ruling

[CLASSICAL POSITION] 
1. **Hijab (General Body Covering):** It is **Fard** (obligatory) for a free adult woman to cover her entire body except for her face, hands, and feet (according to the most correct view in the school) in the presence of non-mahram men.
2. **Niqab (Face Covering):** In the foundational Hanafi texts, the face is **not** part of the *'awrah* (nakedness). Therefore, uncovering it is inherently permissible (*mubah*). However, the dominant later position (mu'tamad) is that it is **Wajib** (obligatory) for a woman to cover her face in public or in the presence of strangers, not because the face is 'awrah, but to prevent *fitna* (temptation/social discord).

**Classical Reference:** *Al-Hidaya* by Al-Marghinani (VERIFIED); *Radd al-Muhtar* by Ibn Abidin (VERIFIED).

---

## Usul al-Fiqh — How This Ruling Was Derived

**Primary Legal Source:** Quran and Sunnah.

**Derivation Chain:**

*If Quran:*
- **Verse:** Surah Al-Nur (Q 24:31) — "...and not display their adornment except that which [necessarily] appears thereof..."
- **Asbab al-Nuzul:** Revealed to establish social etiquette and modesty following incidents of harassment in Medina.
- **Textual Analysis:**
  - *Muhkam or Mutashabih:* Muhkam regarding the obligation of covering; the exception "except what appears" is subject to interpretation.
  - *Amm or Khass:* Amm (general) for all believing free women.
  - *Nasikh/Mansukh:* No abrogation applies here; this verse specifies the earlier general commands of modesty.
- **Juristic Reading:** The Hanafi school interprets "except what appears thereof" as the face and the hands (and feet in some reports), based on the commentary of Ibn Abbas. This establishes that these parts are not *'awrah*.

*If Sunnah:*
- **Hadith:** "When a girl reaches the age of menstruation, it is not right that any of her should be seen except this and this — and he pointed to his face and hands." — narrated by Aisha (ra), reported in Abu Dawud (VERIFIED).
- **Authentication Status:** Often cited as *Mursal* (chain missing a link), but the Hanafi school accepts it because it is supported by the general practice and the Quranic exception.
- **Asbab al-Wurud:** The Prophet (pbuh) was instructing Asma bint Abi Bakr on appropriate attire.
- **Scope:** General for all adult women.
- **Application:** This hadith serves as the primary textual evidence that the face and hands are excluded from the *'awrah*.

*If Qiyas / Istihsan:*
- **Original Case (Asl):** The prohibition of looking at the 'awrah.
- **New Case (Far'):** Looking at the face of a woman.
- **Effective Cause (Illah):** *Fitna* (temptation).
- **Extended Ruling (Hukm):** While the face is not 'awrah, looking at it with desire is prohibited. By **Istihsan** (juristic preference), later Hanafi jurists ruled that since the prevention of *fitna* is a Sharia objective (Maqsad), and the face is the locus of beauty, covering it becomes **Wajib** (obligatory as a precautionary measure) in times/places where moral corruption is prevalent.
- **Why This Illah:** Hanafis distinguish between the *essential* ruling (the face is not 'awrah) and the *circumstantial* ruling (covering it is required to block the means to sin — *Sadd al-Dhara'i*).

**Usul-Level Disputes:**
The Shafi'i and Hanbali schools generally consider the entire body, including the face and hands, as *'awrah* in the presence of strangers. The Hanafi school diverges by using a more literalist reading of the "exception" in Q 24:31 and prioritizing the practical necessity (*darurah*) of women needing their hands and faces for daily transactions and movement.

---

## Internal Dissent

**Opinion of Abu Yusuf:** He famously included the forearms as part of what may be uncovered if there is a need, though the dominant position limits this to the hands.
**Status of the Feet:** There is internal debate; the *Zahir al-Riwayah* (dominant transmission) is that feet are not 'awrah because of the difficulty (*mashaqqah*) in covering them during movement, while some scholars consider them 'awrah.

---

## Notes

In modern Hanafi practice (South Asia/Turkey), the distinction between "the face is not 'awrah" and "it is wajib to cover the face due to fitna" is often collapsed into a single ruling of obligation for the Niqab in public. However, in judicial testimony (Qada), the face must be uncovered for identification, illustrating the original Hanafi principle that the face is not 'awrah.
