# Hanafi Position: Forex trading in XAUUSD (Gold/USD)
[[T.O.C (Fiqh)|Up to Fiqh]] | [[Synthesis - forex-trading-xauusd-halal|Back to Synthesis]]

**School:** Hanafi
**Methodological Disposition:** Rationalist. Heaviest use of qiyas and istihsan of all four schools.
**Query Type:** MIXED (Applying classical Sarf principles to contemporary digital markets)

---

## Ruling

[DERIVED POSITION]
Trading XAUUSD (Gold against the US Dollar) is **permissible** only if four strict conditions are met simultaneously. If any one condition is violated, the transaction becomes *Riba al-Nasi'ah* (interest of delay) or *Gharar* (uncertainty), making it strictly prohibited (*Haram*).

1. **Immediate Possession (Taqabud):** The exchange must be "hand-to-hand" in the legal sense. In digital trading, this requires "Constructive Possession" (*Qabd Hukmi*), where the gold is instantly credited to the buyer's account and the dollars to the seller's, with the buyer assuming full ownership risk immediately.
2. **Elimination of Interest (Swap-Free):** The trader must use an "Islamic Account" that does not charge or pay "Swap" or "Rollover" fees (interest on overnight positions).
3. **Avoidance of CFDs:** The contract must involve the actual purchase/sale of the underlying asset (Gold) or its specific allocation. Most retail forex brokers use CFDs (Contracts for Difference), which are mere bets on price movements without ownership; these are impermissible due to *Gharar* and *Qimar* (gambling).
4. **Physical Backing:** The broker must actually possess the gold being sold or have a binding contract for its delivery.

**Classical Reference:** *Al-Hidaya* by Al-Marghinani (VERIFIED), *Radd al-Muhtar* by Ibn Abidin (VERIFIED) regarding the rules of *Sarf* (currency exchange).

## Usul al-Fiqh — How This Ruling Was Derived

**Primary Legal Source:** Sunnah

**Derivation Chain:**

*If Sunnah:*
- **Hadith:** "Gold for gold, silver for silver... like for like, hand to hand. If these classes differ, then sell as you wish, provided it is hand to hand." — narrated by 'Ubadah bin al-Samit, reported in Sahih Muslim (VERIFIED).
- **Authentication Status:** Sahih (Authentic). This is the foundational text for *Riba* in sales.
- **Asbab al-Wurud:** The Prophet (ﷺ) issued this to regulate the markets of Medina, preventing exploitative bartering and ensuring that intrinsic values (Gold/Silver) were exchanged fairly.
- **Scope:** The Hanafi school treats this as a general (*Amm*) principle for all items that serve as *Thaman* (price/currency).
- **Application:** Since XAU (Gold) and USD (Fiat) are different genera (*jins*) but both function as currency, the requirement of "equality in amount" is dropped, but the requirement of "hand-to-hand" (*Taqabud*) remains mandatory.

*If Qiyas:*
- **Original Case (Asl):** The exchange of Gold for Silver (Classical *Sarf*).
- **New Case (Far'):** The exchange of Gold (XAU) for Fiat Currency (USD).
- **Effective Cause (Illah):** *Thamaniyya* (Absolute Currency-hood). The Hanafi school identifies the shared property as both being mediums of exchange and measures of value.
- **Extended Ruling (Hukm):** The requirement of immediate delivery (*Taqabud*) in the *Majlis al-Aqd* (contract session) is carried from the Gold-Silver exchange to the Gold-Fiat exchange.
- **Why This Illah:** Hanafis argue that paper money (Fiat) has replaced silver as the secondary *Thaman*. Therefore, it inherits the legal status of *Naqdayn* (the two metals). To allow a delay in delivery would open the door to *Riba al-Nasi'ah*.

**Usul-Level Disputes:**
Modern Hanafi scholars (e.g., Mufti Taqi Usmani) emphasize that while *Istihsan* (juristic preference) allows for "Constructive Possession" to replace physical "Hand-to-Hand" delivery in the digital age, it does *not* allow for the total absence of the underlying asset. The Shafi'i and Hanbali schools are often even stricter, sometimes rejecting constructive possession in gold trades altogether if there is any perceived gap in the settlement cycle (T+0 vs T+2).

## Internal Dissent

There is a significant debate among contemporary Hanafi jurists regarding **Leverage**. 
- **Majority View:** Many scholars view the "loan" provided by the broker for leverage as a "loan that brings a benefit" (*Qard bi Manfa'ah*) because the broker requires the trader to trade through them (earning commissions). This is prohibited.
- **Minority View:** Some argue that if the commission is a standard market fee and not a condition of the loan itself, it may be permissible under *Istihsan* to facilitate modern commerce, provided no interest is involved.

## Notes

In the context of XAUUSD, most retail traders fail the *Sarf* requirements because they trade "Spot Gold" on platforms like MetaTrader 4/5, which are almost always CFDs. To make it halal, one must use a provider that offers "Allocated Gold" where the specific ounces are assigned to the user's name immediately upon the transaction, and ensure the account is strictly swap-free. Participation in "Futures" or "Options" for XAUUSD is strictly prohibited in Hanafi fiqh because the exchange is not immediate.
