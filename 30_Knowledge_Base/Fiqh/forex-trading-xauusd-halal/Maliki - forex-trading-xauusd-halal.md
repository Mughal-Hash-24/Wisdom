# Maliki Position: Forex trading in XAUUSD (Gold/USD)
[[T.O.C (Fiqh)|Up to Fiqh]] | [[Synthesis - forex-trading-xauusd-halal|Back to Synthesis]]

**School:** Maliki
**Methodological Disposition:** Empiricist. Unique use of Medinan practice (amal ahl al-Madina) and maslaha mursala as independent legal sources. Strict adherence to the rules of Sarf (currency exchange) to prevent the "pretext" (sadd al-dhara'i) of riba.
**Query Type:** MIXED

---

## Ruling

[DERIVED POSITION] The standard practice of retail Forex trading in XAUUSD (Gold against the US Dollar) is **impermissible (haram)**. This is due to the presence of multiple prohibited elements: *Riba al-Nasi'ah* (delay in exchange of ribawi items), *Riba al-Fadl* (inequality in exchange), and the combination of a loan and a contract (*bay' wa salaf*).

To make such a transaction **permissible (halal)**, the following conditions must be met simultaneously:
1. **Hand-to-Hand Exchange (T+0):** The exchange must be immediate with constructive possession (*qabd hukmi*) occurring at the moment of the contract.
2. **Elimination of Interest (Swap-Free):** There must be no rollover fees or swap interest for positions held overnight.
3. **Removal of Leverage-based Benefit:** If leverage is provided as a loan, the broker cannot benefit from the transaction (e.g., through spreads or commissions that are contingent on the loan), as this violates the rule: "Every loan that brings a benefit is Riba."
4. **Full Backing:** The contract must represent a real spot transaction where the trader actually takes ownership of the underlying asset.

**Classical Reference:** *Al-Muwatta*, Imam Malik; *Al-Mudawwana*, Sahnun (VERIFIED).

## Usul al-Fiqh — How This Ruling Was Derived

**Primary Legal Source:** Sunnah and Amal ahl al-Madina.

**Derivation Chain:**

*If Sunnah:*
- **Hadith:** "Gold for gold, silver for silver... like for like, hand to hand. If these classes differ, then sell as you wish as long as it is hand to hand." — narrated by 'Ubadah b. al-Samit, reported in Sahih Muslim and Al-Muwatta (VERIFIED).
- **Authentication Status:** Sahih.
- **Asbab al-Wurud:** The Prophet (ﷺ) observed people exchanging different qualities of dates or metals and corrected them to ensure the essence of the exchange was not exploited for usury.
- **Scope:** The Maliki school treats this as *Aam* (general). Gold and currency (USD) are both considered *Thaman* (price/value) and are subject to the rules of *Sarf*.
- **Application:** Since XAU (Gold) and USD are both ribawi items (mediums of exchange), any delay in the settlement (*Nasi'ah*) renders the contract invalid and usurious.

*If Amal ahl al-Madina (Medinan Practice):*
- **The Practice:** The market of Medina, as recorded by Imam Malik, strictly enforced the "spot" nature of *Sarf*. Any separation between the two parties before the exchange was finalized was considered a void contract.
- **Why This Source:** Malik argued that the continuous, observable practice of the descendants of the Sahaba in Medina regarding market transactions is a "living Sunnah" that prevents the misinterpretation of individual hadiths. This practice confirms that "constructive possession" must be immediate in currency/gold trades.

*If Maslaha Mursala (Public Interest):*
- **The Interest:** Protecting the integrity of the currency and preventing speculative bubbles that do not involve real asset transfer.
- **Why Unattested:** While the Quran does not mention "Forex," the harm (*mufsadah*) of speculative trading that relies on interest-bearing loans is evident. 
- **Limits:** The Maliki school uses *Sadd al-Dhara'i* (blocking the means) here; even if the trader intends to profit fairly, the structure of the leverage and delay is a "means" to Riba.

**Usul-Level Disputes:**
- The **Hanafi** school allows a slight delay in "constructive possession" for administrative purposes in modern banking (T+2), whereas the Maliki tradition is more stringent on the "majlis al-aqd" (the sitting of the contract) requiring immediate finality for gold.
- The **Hanbali** school (some modern scholars) might be more lenient on the broker's commission in leveraged trades, whereas Malikis see the broker-lender relationship as a prohibited combination of *bay' wa salaf*.

---

## Internal Dissent

There is a minority discussion regarding **Constructive Possession (Qabd Hukmi)**. While the *mu'tamad* (dominant) position requires immediate settlement, some modern Maliki scholars (notably in North African councils) have argued that digital ledger entries in a regulated exchange constitute *qabd* according to *urf* (custom). However, this dissent does not extend to allowing leverage-based interest or swaps.

---

## Notes

In North and West Africa (Maliki strongholds), modern Islamic banking regulations often follow the OIC Fiqh Academy rulings which align with the Maliki caution regarding *Sarf*. "Islamic Forex accounts" are marketed to meet these criteria, but many Maliki jurists remain skeptical of the "Brokerage Commission" being a hidden benefit for the leverage loan.
