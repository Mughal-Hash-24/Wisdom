# Hanbali Position: Forex Trading in XAUUSD
[[T.O.C (Fiqh)|Up to Fiqh]] | [[Synthesis - forex-trading-xauusd-halal|Back to Synthesis]]

**School:** Hanbali
**Methodological Disposition:** Textualist. Extreme strictness regarding the conditions of *Sarf* (currency/precious metal exchange) and the prohibition of combining a loan with a sale (*Qard wa Bay'*).
**Query Type:** [MIXED]

---

## Ruling

[DERIVED POSITION]
Forex trading in XAUUSD (Gold vs. US Dollar) is **Impermissible (Haram)** in its standard retail form due to the violation of several core legal requirements. For such a transaction to be **Permissible (Halal)**, it must strictly satisfy three conditions:
1. **Taqabud (Hand-to-hand exchange):** The exchange must be completed in the same contractual session (*majlis al-aqd*). In modern terms, "constructive possession" (*qabd hukmi*) must result in the immediate settlement of the gold into the buyer's account and the dollars into the seller's.
2. **Elimination of Riba (Interest):** The account must be "Swap-Free," meaning no rollover interest is charged or paid for holding positions overnight.
3. **Avoidance of Prohibited Combinations:** The use of leverage (margin trading) is generally prohibited because it constitutes a loan from the broker conditioned upon a trade, which violates the Prophetic prohibition against combining a loan and a sale.

**Classical Reference:** *Al-Mughni* by Ibn Qudama (VERIFIED) — Section on *Sarf* (Money Exchange).

---

## Usul al-Fiqh — How This Ruling Was Derived

**Primary Legal Source:** Sunnah (Hadith regarding Ribawi goods).

**Derivation Chain:**

*If Sunnah:*
- **Hadith:** "Gold for gold, silver for silver, wheat for wheat, barley for barley, dates for dates, and salt for salt — like for like, equal for equal, and hand-to-hand... if these classes differ, then sell as you wish, provided it is hand-to-hand." — narrated by Ubada bin al-Samit, reported in Sahih Muslim (VERIFIED).
- **Authentication Status:** Sahih.
- **Asbab al-Wurud:** The Prophet (ﷺ) was establishing market regulations in Medina to prevent exploitative practices and economic instability through currency manipulation.
- **Scope:** The Hanbali school treats this as *Amm* (general) for all items serving as a medium of exchange or "price" (*thaman*).
- **Application:** Since XAU (Gold) and USD (Currency/Price) are both Ribawi items used as media of exchange, they fall under the strict rule of *hand-to-hand* exchange. Any delay (*Nasi'ah*) in the delivery of either side constitutes *Riba al-Nasi'ah*.

*If Qiyas (Last resort applied to modern currency):*
- **Why Qiyas Required:** Modern fiat currency (USD) did not exist at the time of revelation.
- **Original Case (Asl):** Silver (Dirham).
- **New Case (Far'):** US Dollar (USD).
- **Effective Cause (Illah):** *Thamaniyyah* (Absolute value/medium of exchange).
- **Extended Ruling (Hukm):** The rules of *Sarf* applying to Silver vs. Gold apply to USD vs. Gold.
- **Why This Illah:** The Hanbali school identifies the *illah* as the inherent quality of being a price/measure of value. Therefore, exchanging Gold for Dollars is legally identical to exchanging Gold for Silver.

**Prohibition of Leverage (The Hanbali Strictness):**
- **Principle:** *Al-ghunm bi-l-ghurm* (Return is linked to risk-bearing).
- **Hadith:** "The Prophet (ﷺ) forbade a loan and a sale in one contract" (*La yahillu salafun wa bay'*). Reported in Sunan Abi Dawud and Tirmidhi (VERIFIED).
- **Usul-Level Dispute:** While some schools might interpret this prohibition as applying only to specific usurious structures, the Hanbali school applies it broadly to any contract where a loan is used as a lever to force a sale through a specific broker. In retail forex, the broker provides a "loan" (leverage) on the condition that you trade (sale) through their platform, which the school views as a prohibited combination (*Qard wa Bay'*).

---

## Internal Dissent

**Ibn Taymiyya's Nuance:** Ibn Taymiyya (a major Hanbali scholar) occasionally allowed for broader definitions of possession (*qabd*) depending on custom (*'urf*). Some modern Hanbali-leaning scholars (like those in the Permanent Committee, KSA) have used this to accept electronic settlement as "constructive possession," provided the settlement is immediate and no interest is involved. However, the dissent regarding leverage remains minimal; most Hanbali authorities view the leverage model of retail brokers as fundamentally flawed under the prohibition of combining a loan and a sale.

---

## Notes

In modern Saudi and Qatari practice, many scholars allow spot gold trading only if the gold is physically allocated to the buyer in a vault (allocated accounts) and the transaction is settled instantly. The 2006 AAOIFI Shariah standards, which are heavily influenced by Hanbali logic regarding *Sarf*, reiterate that "delay" in the transfer of gold or its price renders the contract void. Forex "CFDs" (Contracts for Difference) on XAUUSD are universally regarded as Haram by the Hanbali tradition because no actual exchange of gold occurs; it is merely gambling on price movements.
