# Structure of Ihya' 'Ulum al-Din

[[T.O.C (Psychology_Philosophy)|Up to Psychology & Philosophy]]

## Overview
Al-Ghazali divided the *Ihya* into **four quarters** (*Rub'*), each containing **ten books** (*Kitab*), totaling **forty books**. This structure mirrors the journey from outward action to inner purification.

## 1. The Quarter of Acts of Worship (Rub' al-'Ibadat)
*Focus: The inner dimensions and spiritual secrets of the "Five Pillars" and other rituals.*
1.  **Book of Knowledge:** The foundation of all religious life.
2.  **Articles of Faith:** Creed and theology.
3.  **Mysteries of Purity:** Physical and spiritual cleanliness.
4.  **Mysteries of Prayer:** The connection with the Divine.
5.  **Mysteries of Alms-giving (Zakat):** Purification of wealth.
6.  **Mysteries of Fasting:** Discipline of the appetites.
7.  **Mysteries of Pilgrimage (Hajj):** The journey to the Sacred House.
8.  **Etiquette of Qur'an Recitation:** Interacting with the Divine Word.
9.  **Invocations and Supplications:** Remembrance of God (*Dhikr*).
10. **Regular Offices (Wirds):** Managing time for worship.

## 2. The Quarter of Norms of Daily Life (Rub' al-'Adat)
*Focus: Sanctifying mundane daily activities by aligning them with ethical intentions.*
11. **Etiquette of Eating:** Moderation and manners.
12. **Etiquette of Marriage:** Rights, responsibilities, and desire.
13. **Etiquette of Earning a Livelihood:** Business ethics and trust (*Tawakkul*).
14. **The Lawful and Prohibited:** Discernment in consumption.
15. **Duties of Brotherhood/Friendship:** Social bonds and rights.
16. **Etiquette of Seclusion:** The value of solitude vs. society.
17. **Etiquette of Travel:** Physical and spiritual journeying.
18. **Music and Ecstasy:** The psychology of listening (*Sama'*).
19. **Commanding Right and Forbidding Wrong:** Social responsibility.
20. **Etiquette of Living and Prophetic Character:** Following the Sunnah.

## 3. The Quarter of Destructive Vices (Rub' al-Muhlikat)
*Focus: "Diagnosis" of the diseases of the heart that lead to spiritual ruin.*
21. **The Wonders of the Heart:** Understanding the spiritual organ.
22. **Discipline of the Soul:** Improving character.
23. **Breaking the Two Desires:** Gluttony and Lust.
24. **Vices of the Tongue:** Lying, backbiting, slander.
25. **Anger, Hatred, and Envy:** Emotional poisons.
26. **Disparagement of the World:** The root of all error.
27. **Love of Wealth:** Avarice and miserliness.
28. **Love of Status and Ostentation (Riya):** Doing good for praise.
29. **Pride and Vanity:** Arrogance and self-delusion.
30. **Deception (Ghurur):** Spiritual complacency.

## 4. The Quarter of Saving Virtues (Rub' al-Munjiyat)
*Focus: "Cure" and cultivation of the qualities that lead to salvation.*
30. **Repentance (Tawba):** The return to God.
31. **Patience (Sabr) and Gratitude (Shukr):** Balancing adversity and blessing.
32. **Fear (Khawf) and Hope (Raja):** The two wings of the believer.
33. **Poverty (Faqr) and Abstinence (Zuhd):** Detachment from the material.
34. **Monotheism (Tawhid) and Trust (Tawakkul):** Reliance on the One.
35. **Love (Mahabba), Longing, Intimacy, and Contentment:** The highest stations.
36. **Intention, Sincerity, and Truthfulness:** The spirit of action.
37. **Monitoring and Self-Accounting (Muhasaba):** Vigilance over the self.
38. **Contemplation (Tafakkur):** Reflection on creation.
39. **Remembrance of Death:** The ultimate reality check.
